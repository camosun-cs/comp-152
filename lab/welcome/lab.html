Task 1: Getting started

  1. Log on to your lab computer

    What are my credentials?

    Your Camosun ID is used to log in to many online services. A Camosun ID starts with a capital C followed by 7 numbers. Example: C0123456. Your Camosun ID is printed on your student ID card.

    Some services may ask you to log in with your student ID, student number, Colleague # or C-number. Regardless of the wording, these services all require your Camosun ID.

    If you have not done so already, you will be be asked to activate your account when you first log in. At this time, you will choose a password for your account. If you've forgotten your password, go to https://password.camosun.ca/ for assistance.

    If you haven't yet activated your account, your default password is your birthday, formatted MMDDYY.

  2. Open your Camosun network drive (aka "M drive" or "M:") in File Explorer

    How to find M drive
    1. Double-click the Computer icon on your desktop, or single-click the File Explorer icon in the task bar.
    2. In the Network Locations section of the window that opens, double-click the icon labelled (\\intra.camosun.bc.ca\users), usually also labelled (M:)

    You can also find M drive in the sidebar of any File Explorer view under the Computer section.

  3. Create a folder to store all of your assignments and other resources for this course on your M drive.

    Creating folders

    To create a new folder, click the New Folder button near the top of the Explorer window. Immediately after pressing the button, the folder name will be highlighted and can be changed simply by typing the name you want and pressing enter. If you'd like to change its name later, right-click on the folder and choose Rename from the contextual menu.

  4. Create a folder for this lab (eg. Lab-welcome) inside of the folder you created in the previous step, and save any files you create or use during this lab into this folder.

  5. Make sure you can find and launch some basic software, including:

    • a web browser (eg. Internet Explorer or Firefox)
    • Microsoft Word
    • Excel
    • PowerPoint
    • Access
    • a "plain-text" editor (eg. Notepad++ or Brackets)

Task 2: D2L and Survey

The D2L platform will be used to post all assignments and labs, and to collect the majority of your work for grading. Using this tool effectively will be vital to your success in this course!

  1. Log on to D2L in your web browser by navigating to https://online.camosun.ca

  2. Locate the COMP 152 course page and make sure you are familiar with all the major features.

    Major features of D2L
    • News feed
    • Calendar
    • Content browser, containing
      • homework
      • lab assignments
      • lecture slides
      • study guides
      • general course information
    • Quizzes
    • Dropbox
    • Grades
    • Discussions
    • E-Mail
  3. Complete a survey on D2L regarding your educational background, experience and areas of interest in computing, and expectations for this course.

Task 3: A short report

  1. Read the article Information-rich and attention-poor

  2. Prepare a one page (double-spaced) report in Microsoft Word highlighting the key points of the paper.

    Report guidelines

    The focus of this activity should be on the content you provide. You can pick any style that you feel most comfortable with, even point-form if you prefer.

    Use appropriate formatting (headings, bullets, colour, etc) to make your document easy to read.

    Make sure to include your own thoughts on the themes brought up in the paper as well as summarizing the author's position.

    You likely won't have time to finish this activity during the lab today. Check the due date for this assignment on D2L to make sure you do not lose marks for handing it in late!

  3. Submit your document to the drop box in D2L
  4. Be prepared to present your thoughts on the paper in class!

Submission

Task 1 has no deliverables to submit.

The survey in Task 2 should be completed online during the lab period.

Submit your report from Task 3 to the dropbox folder for this lab on D2L.
